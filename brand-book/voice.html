Voice Guidelines

Brand Voice

Warm, knowledgeable, and reassuring — like a trusted family optometrist explaining your vision.

01

Personality Traits

📚

Knowledgeable

Explains without condescension. Uses precise terms but always provides context.

🤝

Welcoming

Warm first contact. Every message feels like greeting a neighbour at the door.

🛡️

Reassuring

Calms anxiety about eye health. Confidence without arrogance.

02

Voice Dimensions

FormalConversational

Slightly formal, never slangy. Professional but approachable.

SeriousWarm

Warmth first. Even clinical messages carry empathy.

TechnicalSimple

Balanced. Uses clinical terms but always with plain-English explanation.

03

Tone by Channel

Website

Authoritative yet inviting

Clear CTAs, educational tone, feature the "Guru" guidance angle.

Social Media

Friendly, educational

Short tips, myth-busting, community engagement. Use local language mix (Hindi/English where appropriate).

In-Store

Personal, respectful

Address by name. Listen first. Explain options like a teacher, not a salesman.

Print / Ads

Refined, trustworthy

Minimal copy. Let the visual do the work. Tagline-driven. Emphasize tradition + precision.

WhatsApp / SMS

Concise, caring

Reminder tone: "Your sight matters. Your checkup is in 2 days."

04

Writing Guidelines

Do

  • ✓Use "we" and "you" — personal, direct
  • ✓Explain the "why" behind every recommendation
  • ✓Mix clinical accuracy with everyday language
  • ✓End with care: "Take care of your eyes"
  • ✓Use active voice: "We recommend" not "It is recommended"

Don't

  • ✗Use jargon without explanation: "You have astigmatism" + no context
  • ✗Sound like a corporation — never "we value your patronage"
  • ✗Fear-monger about eye health to drive sales
  • ✗Use slang, emojis in clinical contexts, or overly casual language
  • ✗Be generic — "your vision is our priority" without specifics
